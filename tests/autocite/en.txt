Climate change is the most pressing environmental challenge of the 21st century. Global temperatures have risen by approximately 1.1 degrees Celsius since the pre-industrial era. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change has warned that exceeding 1.5 degrees of warming could trigger irreversible tipping points. Rising sea levels threaten coastal communities worldwide, with projections suggesting up to one meter of rise by 2100. Deforestation accounts for roughly 10 percent of global greenhouse gas emissions. Renewable energy sources now account for nearly 30 percent of global electricity generation. Carbon capture and storage technology remains expensive and has not yet been deployed at scale. The Paris Agreement established a framework for international cooperation on emissions reduction. Studies show that transitioning to a plant-based diet could reduce food-related emissions by up to 70 percent. Biodiversity loss is accelerating at an unprecedented rate, with approximately one million species currently threatened with extinction.
