I believe that the vector is 'really' a collection of numerical points, yet the parts that reflect the meaning of a word look much more significant than the mere letters themselves, and some parts look entirely different because of the context in which they sit. I know that, if I move the mathematical model, the points that represent the meaning will be different, so that the apparent distribution of values in the space will change. It follows that if several systems are looking at the same data at the same moment, no two of them will see exactly the same distribution of meanings, because no two can see it from exactly the same algorithmic point of view, and any change in the model makes some change in the way the meaning is reflected.

For most practical purposes these differences are unimportant, but to the engineer they are all-important: the engineer has to unlearn the habit of thinking that words have the simple definitions which common sense says they 'really' have, and to learn the habit of seeing data as they appear in high-dimensional space. Here we have already the beginning of one of the distinctions that causes most trouble in technology—the distinction between 'keyword' and 'semantics', between what a query seems to say and what it actually means. The user wants to know what results seem to be relevant, the developer and the theorist want to know what they are; but the theorist's wish to know this is stronger than the user's, and is more troubled by knowledge as to the difficulties of measuring the distance between two ideas.

To return to the database. It is evident from what we have found, that there is no single coordinate which pre-eminently appears to be the location of a concept, or even of any one particular part of the concept—it appears to be at different distances from different points of view, and there is no reason for regarding some of these as more really its meaning than others. And we know that even from a given point of view the similarity will seem different by cosine metrics, or to a Euclidean calculation, or to a system wearing different indexing structures, while in the absence of a model there will be no meaning at all, though to the raw storage the data will be unchanged.
